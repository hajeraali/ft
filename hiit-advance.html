High knee

High knees, often incorporated in warm-up routines or as a cardio exercise, are an effective way to boost your heart rate and improve your lower body strength and coordination. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin by standing with your feet hip-width apart and your arms at your sides. Take a moment to prepare for the exercise.
  2. Engage your core muscles to maintain good posture with your chest up and your back straight.
  3. Start marching in place, lifting your knees as high as you comfortably can with each step. Alternate between your right and left legs.
  4. Swing your arms in sync with your knee lifts, as if you are jogging in place. This adds an extra element of cardio intensity.
  5. Continue to lift your knees and march in place at a brisk pace, aiming for a steady rhythm and maintaining good form throughout the exercise.
  6. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Fast punches

This exercise focuses on rapid and controlled punches, engaging your arm, shoulder, and core muscles for a full-body workout. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and your fists guarding your face. Assume a boxer's stance with one foot forward and the other foot back.
  2. Keep your core engaged and maintain good posture with your back straight and chest up.
  3. Begin throwing quick, controlled punches with your leading hand (the one in front), keeping your rear hand (the one at the back) close to your face to protect it.
  4. Alternate between punches, making sure to fully extend your arm and rotate your hip and shoulder with each punch for maximum power and stability.
  5. Continue punching at a rapid pace, focusing on controlled speed rather than force. Keep your breathing steady and stay light on your feet.
  6. You can practice different punch variations, such as jabs, crosses, hooks, and uppercuts, to work on different muscle groups and maintain variety in your routine.
  7. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Step back Front kick

The step back front kick is a martial arts-inspired exercise that targets your lower body, helping to strengthen your legs and improve balance and flexibility. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your hands in a guarding position or at your sides. Take a moment to prepare for the exercise.
  2. Take a step back with your right foot, shifting your weight onto your left leg while maintaining your balance.
  3. As you step back, simultaneously raise your right knee to hip level in front of you.
  4. Extend your right leg forward in a powerful front kick, keeping your toes flexed and your core engaged.
  5. Retract your right leg and return it to the starting position, stepping back with your right foot again to complete one repetition.
  6. Repeat the step back front kick with your left leg by stepping back with your left foot, raising your left knee, and delivering a front kick.
  7. Continue to alternate between the right and left legs for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining good posture and breathing steadily throughout the exercise.
  8. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Sumo squat + Punch

It targets your legs, glutes, and core, while also engaging your upper body and helping improve coordination and balance. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart and your toes turned slightly outward. Place your hands in fists near your chest or face, as if you're preparing to punch.
  2. Engage your core muscles to maintain an upright posture with your chest up and your back straight.
  3. Bend your knees and hips to lower your body into a sumo squat, pushing your hips back as you go down. Keep your knees in line with your feet, and aim to get your thighs parallel to the ground.
  4. As you rise from the squat, simultaneously punch your right arm straight out in front of you, extending your arm fully.
  5. Lower your arm back to the guard position as you return to the squat position.
  6. Repeat the sumo squat and punch, this time using your left arm for the punch. Alternate between right and left punches with each squat.
  7. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Standing knee to elbow

he standing knee to elbow exercise is a dynamic movement that combines balance, core strength, and flexibility. It's an effective exercise for targeting the abdominal muscles and improving overall body coordination. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin by standing with your feet hip-width apart and your arms at your sides. Take a moment to find your balance and prepare for the exercise.
  2. Engage your core muscles and maintain an upright posture with your chest up and your back straight.
  3. Raise your right knee as high as you comfortably can while simultaneously bending your torso to the right.
  4. Try to touch your right elbow to your right knee, or get as close as your flexibility allows. You'll feel a strong contraction in your side oblique muscles.
  5. Return your right knee and torso to the starting position, keeping your balance and control.
  6. Repeat the movement with your left knee, bending your torso to the left and trying to touch your left elbow to your left knee.
  7. Continue to alternate between your right and left sides, aiming for a steady and controlled movement.
  8. Exhale as you bring your knee to your elbow, and inhale as you return to the starting position.
  9. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Speed Bag punches

This workout focuses on enhancing hand-eye coordination, shoulder strength, and rhythm Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing in a comfortable boxing stance with your feet shoulder-width apart. Place your dominant hand (if you're right-handed, it's your right; if you're left-handed, it's your left) forward.
  2. Raise your dominant hand and keep your non-dominant hand guarding your face. Position yourself in front of the speed bag, with your eyes focused on its center.
  3. Initiate the movement by using your dominant hand to hit the speed bag with a quick, controlled punch. Your punch should be aimed slightly below the center of the bag to create an upward movement.
  4. As the bag rebounds, use your non-dominant hand to hit it with another punch. Your non-dominant punch should also be aimed slightly below the center of the bag, creating an upward and continuous rhythmic motion.
  5. Continue to alternate punches with both hands to maintain the speed bag's rhythm. The goal is to hit the bag in a consistent and repetitive pattern, creating a steady "bounce" of the bag.
  6. As you become more proficient, increase the speed and precision of your punches. The key is to maintain a smooth and controlled motion, hitting the bag with the appropriate part of your fist (not the knuckles) and keeping your wrists relaxed.
  7. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
