Side Lunge

The side lunge is an effective lower body exercise that targets your quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, while also improving hip flexibility and balance. It's a great addition to your workout routine for building strength and mobility. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet together and your hands on your hips or by your sides.
  2. Take a step to the right, extending your right leg to the side.
  3. As you step to the side, bend your right knee, lowering your body into a lunge position.
  4. Keep your left leg straight, and your weight on your right heel.
  5. Ensure your chest is up, back straight, and core engaged throughout the movement.
  6. Push off your right heel to return to the starting position.
  7. Repeat on the opposite side, stepping to the left, bending your left knee, and lunging to that side.
Push Ups

Push-ups are a classic bodyweight exercise that strengthens the upper body, particularly the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin in a plank position with your hands on the ground directly under your shoulders, fingers pointing forward, and your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Engage your core and maintain a neutral spine, ensuring your back is not arched or sagging.
  3. Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your torso, until your chest is just above the ground or as far as your strength allows.
  4. Push through your palms, extending your arms to lift your body back up to the plank position.
  5. Repeat the push-up motion for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form.
  6. Remember to breathe steadily throughout the exercise, exhaling as you push up and inhaling as you lower down.
Plank

The plank is a fundamental core-strengthening exercise that also engages various muscle groups, including the shoulders, back, and legs. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin by getting into a push-up position with your arms straight and directly beneath your shoulders. Your body should form a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Engage your core muscles by pulling your belly button toward your spine, and squeeze your glutes to maintain a straight posture.
  3. Hold this position, ensuring that your head is in line with your spine and your neck is relaxed. Focus on a spot on the floor to maintain proper neck alignment.
  4. Keep your elbows slightly bent to avoid locking them out. Your weight should be on your forearms and toes.
  5. Hold the plank for as long as you can maintain good form, aiming for 20-60 seconds for beginners and longer durations as you progress.
  6. Breathe steadily throughout the exercise. Do not hold your breath.
  7. To release, gently lower your knees to the ground and return to a relaxed position.
Squats

Squats are a fundamental lower-body exercise that targets multiple muscle groups, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward.
  2. Keep your chest up and your back straight, maintaining a natural curve in your spine.
  3. Extend your arms straight out in front of you or place your hands on your hips for balance.
  4. Initiate the squat by pushing your hips back and bending your knees.
  5. Lower your body down as if you're sitting back into a chair, ensuring your knees don't go past your toes.
  6. Keep your weight on your heels and maintain a strong core throughout the movement.
  7. Continue descending until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as your mobility allows.
  8. Push through your heels to stand back up, extending your hips and knees simultaneously.
  9. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, typically 3 sets of 10-15 reps.
Alternating Knee High

The alternating knee high exercise is a dynamic and effective movement that can be incorporated into your workout routine to improve cardiovascular fitness, lower body strength, and balance. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand in the Mountain Pose (Tadasana) with your feet hip-width apart and arms at your sides. Take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  2. Engage your core muscles and lift your right knee as high as you comfortably can, while maintaining balance.
  3. Lower your right knee back to the starting position as you simultaneously raise your left knee as high as possible.
  4. Continue to alternate between lifting your right and left knees in a controlled and rhythmic manner.
  5. Maintain good posture with your back straight and chest up throughout the exercise.
  6. Perform the desired number of repetitions, aiming for a steady and controlled movement.
  7. Finish the exercise and return to the Mountain Pose to cool down and stretch if needed.
