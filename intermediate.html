Cow Face Pose

Cow Face Pose, or Gomukhasana, is a yoga pose that stretches the arms, shoulders, chest, and hips. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Sit with your legs extended.
  2. Cross your right knee over your left knee.
  3. Bring your heels close to your hips.
  4. Extend your left arm up, bend the elbow, and reach it down your back.
  5. Extend your right arm to the side, bend the elbow, and reach it behind your back.
  6. Clasp your fingers behind your back
  7. Open your chest, breathe deeply, and hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute
  8. Switch your arms and repeat the pose.
Half Bow Pose

The Half Bow Pose, or Ardha Dhanurasana, is a yoga pose that involves a backbend and a chest opening.It can help improve flexibility and mobility in the spine and back. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by lying on your stomach.
  2. Bend one knee and reach back to hold the ankle.
  3. Inhale and kick the foot into your hand, lifting your head, chest, and the opposite leg.
  4. Create a gentle backbend and hold for a few breaths.
  5. Lower yourself back down.
  6. Switch to the other side, bending the opposite knee and repeating the lift.
Half Lord of Fishes Pose

Half Lord of Fishes Pose or Ardha Matsyendrasana is an excellent pose for stretching and revitalizing the spine, hips, and shoulders. It also aids in digestion and detoxification. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start seated with legs extended.
  2. Bend your knees and place feet flat on the floor.
  3. Cross right foot over the left leg.
  4. Keep left foot on the outside of the right thigh.
  5. Inhale, raise your left arm.
  6. Exhale, twist to the right, hooking your left elbow outside the right knee.
  7. Look over your right shoulder.
  8. Hold and breathe for a few breaths.
  9. Release and switch sides, crossing your left foot over the right leg and twisting to the left.
Criss-Cross

Criss-Cross is an exercise in Pilates that targets the abdominal muscles and the obliques. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the mat.
  2. Lift your head and chest off the mat, supporting your head with your hands.
  3. Lift both feet off the mat, knees at a 90-degree angle.
  4. Exhale as you twist, bringing your left armpit to your right knee and extending the left leg.
  5. Inhale as you switch sides, bringing your right armpit to your left knee and extending the right leg.
  6. Continue the twisting motion with a rhythmic breath pattern.
  7. Perform 6-8 full twists (3-4 on each side).
Mountain Climber

Mountain Climbers are a core and full-body exercise emphasizing control and balance. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin in a high plank position.
  2. Lift one foot off the ground, keeping your knee bent.
  3. Bring your knee toward the corresponding elbow.
  4. Extend the leg back to the starting position.
  5. Alternate sides in a controlled manner.
  6. Aim for 10-15 repetitions on each side.
