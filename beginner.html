Tree Pose

The Tree Pose, or "Vrikshasana" in yoga, is a balancing pose that can help improve your balance, concentration, and posture. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand in the Mountain Pose (Tadasana) with your feet hip-width apart and arms at your sides. Take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
  2. Shift your weight to your right foot.
  3. Bring your left foot to the inside of your right thigh or calf, avoiding the knee joint.
  4. Keep your hands in prayer position or overhead and engage your core, keep hips level, and maintain a tall spine.
  5. Focus on your breath and a fixed point (a spot on the floor or wall, to help with balance and concentration)
  6. Hold for 30 seconds to a minute.
  7. Lower your foot and return to Mountain Pose and repeat on the other side.
Downward Facing Dog

Downward Facing Dog is an excellent pose for stretching the entire body and building strength. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin in a tabletop position on your hands and knees. Your hands should be directly under your shoulders, and your knees should be under your hips.
  2. Lift your hips up, straightening your legs and forming an inverted "V" shape.
  3. Spread your fingers wide, engage your core, and press heels toward the floor.
  4. Keep your head between your arms and breathe deeply.
  5. Hold for 30 seconds to a minute, then bend your knees and return to tabletop.
  6. Focus on creating a sense of length and space in your spine, rather than forcing your heels down.
Reverse Warrior

Reverse Warrior, also known as Viparita Virabhadrasana, is a yoga pose that combines a deep side stretch with a gentle backbend. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start in a standing position (Tadasana) at the top of your mat.
  2. Take a step back with your right foot, toes angled outward.
  3. Extend your arms out at shoulder height, left knee bent over the left ankle.
  4. Reach your left hand down your left leg and extend your right arm overhead.
  5. Gaze up at your right hand and gently arch your upper back.
  6. Hold for 15-30 seconds, then return to a standing position.
  7. Switch sides and repeat the pose.
The Roll Up

The Roll Up is an effective Pilates exercise for strengthening the core, improving flexibility, and promoting overall spinal mobility. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start lying on your back with legs extended and arms overhead.
  2. Inhale, engage your core, and exhale as you roll up, reaching for your toes.
  3. Inhale at the top, sitting tall and folding forward.
  4. Exhale as you roll back down one vertebra at a time, keeping legs straight.
  5. Repeat 6-8 times, focusing on control and core engagement
