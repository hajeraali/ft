Air Squats

This exercise is excellent for building lower body strength, improving mobility, and enhancing overall functional fitness. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart, ensuring your toes are pointed slightly outward. Your arms can be positioned in front of you or placed on your hips or behind your head for balance.
  2. Initiate the squat by pushing your hips back and bending your knees. Imagine you're sitting back into an imaginary chair.
  3. Lower your body down, ensuring that your knees stay in line with your feet and don't go past your toes.
  4. Continue descending until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as your mobility allows. Maintain a strong core and a neutral spine throughout the movement.
  5. Push through your heels to stand back up, extending your hips and knees simultaneously. Exhale as you rise back to the starting position.
  6. Repeat the squat for the desired number of repetitions, typically 3 sets of 10-15 reps, or as part of a comprehensive workout routine.
  7. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Skater jumps

Skater jumps, also known as lateral jumps or side-to-side jumps, are an excellent plyometric exercise that focuses on improving lateral strength, balance, and agility. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet hip-width apart and your arms at your sides. Find your balance and maintain good posture.
  2. Engage your core muscles to stabilize your body and keep your chest up with your back straight.
  3. Shift your weight to your right foot and jump to the left, landing on your left foot with your right foot crossing behind you.
  4. As you land on your left foot, your right foot should be a few inches above the ground, and your left knee should be bent at a 90-degree angle.
  5. Immediately jump back to the right, landing on your right foot with your left foot crossing behind you. Keep the movement fluid and controlled.
  6. Continue to alternate between left and right jumps, maintaining a side-to-side rhythm. Aim to land softly and absorb the impact with your leg muscles.
  7. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Push Up Push Back

The "push-up push-back" is a challenging and effective variation of the standard push-up exercise. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin in a standard push-up position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, and your body forming a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Lower your body down into a push-up by bending your elbows and keeping them close to your body. Your chest should come close to the ground.
  3. Push through your palms to extend your arms and return to the starting push-up position.
  4. From the push-up position, push your hips back and shift your weight onto your heels, bending at your waist to form an inverted "V" shape with your body, similar to the downward dog yoga pose.
  5. Hold the downward dog position briefly to stretch and engage your shoulders and hamstrings.
  6. Return to the starting push-up position and repeat the sequence, performing a push-up, and then pushing your hips back into the downward dog position.
  7. Exhale as you push back into the downward dog and inhale as you return to the push-up position. Continue this sequence for the desired number of repetitions.
  8. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Water balls

It is a classic bodyweight exercise that strengthens the upper body, particularly the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and your arms at your sides, ensuring a stable and balanced position.
  2. Engage your core muscles to maintain proper posture and balance throughout the exercise.
  3. Bend your knees and lower your body into a squat position, simultaneously swinging your arms back for added momentum.
  4. Perform an explosive jump by extending your hips and knees, while simultaneously swinging your arms forward to gain maximum height.
  5. At the peak of your jump, tuck your knees toward your chest to enhance your height and control in the air.
  6. Land softly on the balls of your feet, ensuring that your knees are slightly bent to absorb the impact and maintain balance.
  7. Immediately transition into the next jump by pushing off from the ground with another explosive jump, maintaining a steady rhythm.
  8. Exhale as you jump and inhale as you land, continuing to perform these jumping exercises for the desired number of repetitions as part of your HIIT workout.
  9. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
Squat jacks

Squat jacks are a dynamic and high-intensity exercise that combines the benefits of squats and jumping jacks Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Begin by standing with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Take a moment to prepare for the exercise.
  2. Engage your core muscles and maintain an upright posture with your chest up and your back straight.
  3. Perform a standard squat by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, as if you're sitting into an imaginary chair. Ensure your knees are in line with your feet.
  4. As you rise back up from the squat, jump into the air and spread your legs apart while simultaneously raising your arms out to the sides (similar to a jumping jack motion).
  5. Land softly with your knees slightly bent to absorb the impact, immediately transitioning into the next squat, and then jump again into the next squat jack.
  6. Continue to repeat this cycle, squatting and then jumping into the air with the jack motion, for the desired number of repetitions or as part of a larger workout routine.
  7. Breathe steadily throughout the exercise, exhaling as you jump and inhaling as you land and squat.
  8. Once done, Take a 10 seconds break.
