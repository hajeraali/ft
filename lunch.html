Chickpea and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (canned or cooked from dried)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g., bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snow peas)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce (or tamari for a gluten-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey (or maple syrup for a vegan option)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked brown rice or quinoa for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sauce: If using canned chickpeas, rinse and drain them. Chop your mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Mince the garlic and ginger. Chop the onion

  2. Stir-Fry the Vegetables:Heat the olive oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes until they are tender but still crisp. You can adjust the cooking time based on your preferred level of doneness.

  3. Add Chickpeas and Sauce:Add the cooked chickpeas to the pan and pour the sauce over the vegetables and chickpeas. Stir everything together and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the chickpeas are heated through, and the sauce is well incorporated. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. To Serve:Serve the chickpea and vegetable stir-fry over cooked brown rice or quinoa.
