Full sit up

A full sit-up, also known as a standard sit-up, is a classic and effective abdominal exercise that targets the muscles of your core. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
  2. Cross your arms over your chest or place your hands behind your head, with your elbows pointing out.
  3. Engage your core muscles to lift your upper body off the ground, curling your torso towards your knees.
  4. Continue to rise until your elbows or hands touch or come close to your thighs.
  5. Lower your upper body back down to the starting position, keeping your feet on the ground.
  6. Repeat the motion for the desired number of sit-ups, ensuring proper form and control throughout.
Alternating hand swing

  1. Start in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Hold a weight (such as a dumbbell or kettlebell) in both hands, palms facing your body.
  3. Engage your core and hinge at your hips while keeping your back straight.
  4. Swing the weight backward between your legs, maintaining a slight bend in your knees.
  5. Thrust your hips forward explosively, swinging the weight up to chest level.
  6. At the top of the swing, the weight should be at shoulder height, and your arms should be fully extended.
  7. Control the descent of the weight as it swings back down between your legs.
  8. Repeat the swing in a fluid, continuous motion, alternating between hands with each repetition.
  9. Maintain a strong and stable core throughout the exercise.
Glute bridge

This simple yet effective exercise involves lifting the hips off the ground, creating a bridge-like position, and is widely used to enhance glute strength, improve hip stability, and support lower body functionality. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
  2. Place your arms by your sides, palms facing down for stability.
  3. Engage your core muscles to support your lower back.
  4. Press through your heels and lift your hips off the ground, creating a straight line from your shoulders to your knees.
  5. Squeeze your glutes at the top and hold the position for a moment.
  6. Lower your hips back to the ground in a controlled manner, and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
Straight leg Russian twists

Straight-leg Russian twists are a challenging core exercise that target the obliques and help improve overall core strength and stability. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet together.
  2. Sit on the floor with your knees slightly bent and your feet flat, creating a V-shape with your legs.
  3. Lean back slightly to engage your core, keeping your back straight and chest lifted.
  4. Lift your feet off the ground and extend your legs straight in front of you, balancing on your sit bones.
  5. Hold a weight or a medicine ball with both hands, and twist your torso to the right, bringing the weight toward the floor next to your right hip.
  6. Return to the center, and then twist to the left, bringing the weight next to your left hip. This completes one repetition.
Bend over row

Bent-over rows help improve posture, increase back strength, and enhance overall upper body muscular development. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell or dumbbells in front of your thighs.
  2. Hinge at your hips and bend your knees slightly to lower your torso forward, keeping your back straight and chest up.
  3. Hold the weights with your palms facing your body, arms fully extended.
  4. Engage your back muscles, pull the weights towards your lower ribs, and squeeze your shoulder blades together.
  5. Lower the weights back to the starting position, keeping a controlled movement.
  6. Repeat the exercise for the desired number of repetitions.
