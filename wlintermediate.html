Split Squats

Split squats target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and even the core muscles, offering numerous benefits for overall lower body strength and stability Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet together.
  2. Take a step forward with one foot and a step back with the other, creating a staggered stance.
  3. Lower your body by bending both knees, keeping your front knee aligned with your ankle.
  4. Ensure your back knee hovers just above the ground or lightly touches it.
  5. Push through your front heel to return to the starting position.
  6. Perform the desired number of reps, then switch the position of your legs to work the opposite side.
Dumbbells to floor sumo squats

Dumbbells to floor sumo squats are a dynamic lower body exercise that combines the wide stance of a sumo squat with the added challenge of lowering dumbbells to the floor, providing an effective way to strengthen the legs, glutes, and core while improving flexibility. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes turned out at an angle.
  2. Hold a dumbbell with both hands in front of your body, arms extended.
  3. Lower the dumbbell to the floor by bending your knees and pushing your hips back.
  4. Go as low as your flexibility allows or until the dumbbell touches the floor.
  5. Push through your heels to stand back up, straightening your knees and hips.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, keeping your back straight and chest up.
Goblet Duck walk

This exercise involves holding a dumbbell or kettlebell close to your chest while moving in a duck-walk pattern, making it a challenging full-body workout. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Start by holding a dumbbell or kettlebell close to your chest with both hands, keeping your elbows tucked in.
  2. Begin with your feet hip-width apart and toes pointed slightly outward.
  3. Lower yourself into a squat position by bending your knees and hips, keeping your back straight and chest up.
  4. While in the squat position, take small steps forward, maintaining the depth of your squat as you move.
  5. Continue the duck walk for a set distance or a specified number of steps, keeping your core engaged and maintaining good posture throughout.
  6. To end the exercise, stand up from the squat position, and carefully place the weight back down.
Reach up + down

The "Reach Up + Down" exercise is a simple yet effective movement that promotes flexibility and mobility in the upper and lower body. Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your arms relaxed at your sides.
  2. Inhale as you reach both arms overhead, extending your body upward.
  3. Exhale and hinge at your hips to bend forward, reaching your hands toward the floor.
  4. Keep your back straight and your knees slightly bent as you reach down.
  5. Inhale again and return to the upright position, extending your body upward.
  6. Exhale as you lower your arms to your sides to complete one repetition.
Goblet Squat + Pulse

The goblet squat with a pulse is a dynamic lower-body exercise that combines the standard goblet squat with a controlled, small pulsing movement at the lowest point of the squat, helping to strengthen and tone the legs and glutes Refer the video and follow these steps:

  1. Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell close to your chest with both hands, close to your body.
  2. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward.
  3. Initiate the squat by pushing your hips back and bending your knees, lowering your body toward the ground.
  4. Descend until your thighs are at least parallel to the ground or as far as your flexibility and strength allow.
  5. Hold the bottom position and perform a controlled, small pulsing motion by moving up and down slightly within this range.
  6. Maintain a straight back and keep the weight close to your chest during the pulse.
  7. Rise back up to the starting position by straightening your legs and pushing through your heels.
  8. Repeat the goblet squat with pulses for the desired number of repetitions.
